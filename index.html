What is a Report?

A report is a structured document that presents information, findings, or recommendations about a specific topic, situation, or problem.

  • Purpose: To inform, analyze, and recommend
  • Format: Organized and structured presentation
  • Audience: Specific target readers
  • Content: Facts, data, analysis, and conclusions

Types of Reports

Reports can be classified in various ways based on different criteria:

  • By Purpose: Informational vs Analytical
  • By Format: Formal vs Informal
  • By Direction: Upward, Downward, Lateral
  • By Frequency: Periodic vs Special
  • By Length: Short vs Long

Report Structure

A well-structured report follows a logical organization that guides readers through your content effectively.

üìë Preliminary Parts

Title page, table of contents, executive summary

üìñ Main Body

Introduction, methodology, findings, analysis

üìã Supplementary Parts

Conclusions, recommendations, appendices

üìê

Complete Report Structure

  • Title Page: Report title, author, date, organization
  • Table of Contents: Section headings with page numbers
  • Executive Summary: Brief overview of key points
  • Introduction: Background, purpose, scope
  • Methodology: How data was collected and analyzed
  • Findings: Presentation of data and information
  • Analysis: Interpretation of findings
  • Conclusions: Summary of key insights
  • Recommendations: Suggested actions
  • References: Sources cited
  • Appendices: Supporting materials

Executive Summary

The most important section that busy executives often read first (and sometimes only).

Key Elements:

  • Purpose: Brief statement of report objective
  • Key Findings: Most important discoveries
  • Conclusions: Main insights drawn
  • Recommendations: Primary suggested actions

Length: Usually 1-2 pages (10% of total report length)

Writing Tip: Write this section last, even though it appears first

Memo Reports

Short, informal reports using memorandum format for internal communication.

Memo Report Format:

FROM: Your Name, Title
DATE: Current Date
SUBJECT: Brief description of report topic

Body sections:

  • Introduction/Purpose
  • Discussion/Findings
  • Conclusion/Recommendations

Writing Effective Reports

  • Know Your Audience: Understand readers' knowledge level and needs
  • Clear Purpose: Define objective before writing
  • Logical Organization: Present information in logical sequence
  • Concise Writing: Use clear, direct language
  • Visual Elements: Include charts, graphs, tables where helpful
  • Accurate Data: Ensure all facts and figures are correct
  • Proofread: Check for errors in grammar, spelling, formatting
  • Action-Oriented: Focus on what readers should do with the information
